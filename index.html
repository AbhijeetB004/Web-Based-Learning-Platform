What do you think
about BCS Theory?

The BCS theory, named after its developers John Bardeen, Leon Cooper, and Robert Schrieffer, is a fundamental theory in condensed matter physics that explains the phenomenon of superconductivity. Proposed in 1957, the BCS theory provides a microscopic explanation for how certain materials can conduct electricity with zero resistance at low temperatures. At the heart of the BCS theory is the concept of Cooper pairs, which are pairs of electrons that form a bound state due to interactions with the crystal lattice vibrations, known as phonons. These phonon-mediated interactions overcome the usual repulsive forces between electrons, allowing them to form pairs with a net attraction. This pairing mechanism explains why superconductivity typically occurs at low temperatures, where the lattice vibrations are minimized, facilitating the formation of Cooper pairs.
